Experiment 5 - Plastic Where!?

In this experiment I vizualized the NOAA marine microplastics concentation dataset on a virtual globe. The pink dots scattered over the globe reflect the relative concentration of microplastics found in our ocean. What's really interesting is how you can see where these microplastics get caught in swirling ocean currents, creating zones of high concentation in areas far away from population centers. Areas like nort-eastern canada have lots of microplastics, despite almost no plastic being generated nearby.

Innovate

After getting the higrez map looking nice, I combined it with the blue marble global imagery from NASA in photoshop and tweeked the colors. I used the sphere renederer from experiment 5, but turned it inside out and improved the mouse controlls. Finally I paired the texture with some homemade twinkle effects in the fragment shader to give it some life.

Reflection

My original goal was to use noaa weather data to show wave heights as a displacement map across the globe. However it proved to be difficult to get updated swell data in a format that was machine readable and easy to turn into a height map. Perhaps the most frustrating parts of this challenge were dealing with dataset differences & geographic projection misaligninments.
