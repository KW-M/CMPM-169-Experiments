Experiment 5 - Our Home

In this experiment I played with webgl vertex shaders. I initially based it on the P5 Shader 3d Examples, especially the displacement texture and normal displacement examples.

Imitate

Originally I tried to use the depth buffer to give the computed displacement a nicer SSAO look while maintaining the nice glowing effect of simply displaying the normals. Later I decided to try the texture displacement with equirectangular textures for the sphere. This looked great, but I learned that the p5 sphere generator has a resolution limit that is quite low. Instead I used a hi-rez iso sphere from blemder to get better detail from the height map.

Integrate

Combining the Texture displacement with the sinwave based displacement gave an interesting effect. By modulating the sin waves based on the terrain of the globe, moutanous regions become more active, while the sea became smooth.

Innovate

At somepoint I was trying to find ways of mathematically separating areas of the map within the vertex shader (while avoiding costly if statements). One of the optimizations I tried was using the reverse calculated position of each point in the fragment shader to selectively render pixels behind a certain depth. This gave the apperance of a hollowed out earth or blob like shapes, vaugely reminicient of landmasses (depending on where the cuttof was). I liked the look, so I tweeked the depth masks and added a slight depth gradient at the edges, to make it look as if the blobs of earth are emerging from a black soup or mist.

Reflection

I enjoyed the aesthetics of this challenge more than the last. Since I had some experience with shader writing it was easier to get closer to the results I wanted than with the previous experiments, although still challenging and frustrating at times.
